What is a Proforma Invoice? When and How to Use One

7 min read | Updated April 2026

A proforma invoice is a preliminary bill of sale sent to buyers before goods or services are delivered. Unlike regular invoices, proforma invoices are quotes or estimates that outline the expected costs, terms, and details of a future transaction without demanding immediate payment.

Think of a proforma invoice as a formal quote that looks like a real invoice. It serves as a commitment from the seller about pricing and terms, helping buyers understand exactly what they'll receive and how much they'll pay. This document is particularly valuable in international trade, custom orders, and complex business transactions.

Understanding Proforma Invoices vs. Regular Invoices

The key difference between proforma invoices and standard invoices lies in their purpose and legal standing:

  • Regular invoices are legal documents demanding payment for goods or services already delivered or agreed upon
  • Proforma invoices are preliminary documents showing what a transaction will look like if the buyer proceeds
  • Regular invoices create a legal obligation to pay, while proforma invoices do not
  • Proforma invoices can be modified or canceled without legal consequences
  • Regular invoices are recorded as accounts receivable; proforma invoices are not

Legal and Accounting Differences

From an accounting perspective, proforma invoices don't impact your books until they're converted to actual invoices. They're essentially sophisticated quotes that help both parties understand the transaction before committing. This makes them invaluable for:

  • Budget planning and approval processes
  • Securing financing or loans
  • Customs documentation for international shipments
  • Internal approval workflows in large organizations

When to Use a Proforma Invoice

Proforma invoices are particularly useful in specific business scenarios:

International Trade

In international business, proforma invoices are often required for:

  • Customs clearance: Providing detailed product information and values for import/export documentation
  • Letter of credit applications: Banks require proforma invoices to process trade finance
  • Foreign exchange planning: Helping buyers budget for currency conversions
  • Shipping arrangements: Freight forwarders need detailed information for logistics planning

Custom Orders and Manufacturing

When dealing with custom products or services, proforma invoices help:

  • Lock in pricing for complex, multi-component orders
  • Provide detailed specifications and costs before production begins
  • Allow customers to secure internal approvals before committing
  • Establish clear expectations about delivery times and terms

Large Enterprise Sales

B2B transactions often require proforma invoices for:

  • Procurement processes that require multiple approvals
  • Budget allocation and planning cycles
  • Vendor qualification and evaluation processes
  • Contract negotiations and terms discussion

Essential Elements of a Proforma Invoice

A well-structured proforma invoice should include all the elements of a regular invoice, clearly marked as "proforma." Here are the essential components:

Header Information

  • "PROFORMA INVOICE" clearly labeled at the top
  • Your company name, address, and contact information
  • Logo and branding elements
  • Proforma invoice number for tracking
  • Issue date and validity period

Customer Details

  • Customer name and billing address
  • Shipping address if different
  • Contact person and details
  • Customer reference number or purchase order

Product or Service Information

This section should be as detailed as possible:

  • Detailed description of goods or services
  • Quantities, unit prices, and total amounts
  • Product codes, model numbers, or specifications
  • Weight and dimensions for shipping
  • Country of origin for customs purposes

Terms and Conditions

  • Payment terms and methods
  • Delivery terms (Incoterms for international trade)
  • Estimated delivery dates
  • Validity period of the proforma invoice
  • Cancellation or modification policies

How to Create a Professional Proforma Invoice

Creating an effective proforma invoice requires attention to detail and professional presentation. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Template

Select a professional template that includes all necessary fields. Many online invoice generators, including MakeInvoice.online, offer customizable templates specifically designed for proforma invoices.

Step 2: Gather Complete Information

Before creating the document, collect:

  • Complete customer information and requirements
  • Detailed product specifications and pricing
  • Shipping and handling costs
  • Applicable taxes and duties
  • Terms and conditions specific to the transaction

Step 3: Add Professional Touches

  • Include your company logo and branding
  • Use consistent formatting and professional fonts
  • Add clear section headings and organized layout
  • Include contact information for questions
  • Add disclaimers about pricing validity

Step 4: Review and Quality Check

Before sending, verify:

  1. All calculations are accurate
  2. Customer information is correct
  3. Terms and conditions are clear
  4. Contact information is current
  5. The document is clearly marked as "PROFORMA"

Best Practices for Proforma Invoices

To maximize the effectiveness of your proforma invoices, follow these best practices:

Be Transparent About Limitations

  • Clearly state the validity period
  • Explain what could cause pricing changes
  • Include terms for order modifications
  • Specify minimum order quantities if applicable

Maintain Professional Communication

  • Follow up within reasonable timeframes
  • Be responsive to questions and clarifications
  • Provide additional documentation when requested
  • Keep records of all proforma invoices issued

Track and Manage Effectively

Implement a system to:

  • Track which proforma invoices are pending decisions
  • Monitor expiration dates and send reminders
  • Convert approved proforma invoices to regular invoices promptly
  • Archive expired or canceled proforma invoices properly

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these common pitfalls when creating proforma invoices:

  • Not clearly marking as "PROFORMA" - This can cause confusion and legal issues
  • Including incomplete information - Missing details can delay approvals and decisions
  • Setting unrealistic validity periods - Too short may pressure customers; too long may hurt your pricing flexibility
  • Forgetting to include terms - Unclear terms lead to disputes and misunderstandings
  • Not following up appropriately - Lost opportunities due to poor communication

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a proforma invoice and a quote?

While both are preliminary documents, a proforma invoice is more formal and detailed, resembling an actual invoice. Quotes are typically simpler and focus mainly on pricing. Proforma invoices include comprehensive transaction details, terms, and conditions, making them suitable for complex transactions and international trade.

Can I change prices after sending a proforma invoice?

Yes, you can modify prices on a proforma invoice since it's not a legally binding payment demand. However, it's professional to explain any changes to your customer and issue a revised proforma invoice with a new number and date. Always include terms about potential price changes in your original document.

How long should a proforma invoice be valid?

Typical validity periods range from 15-90 days, depending on your industry and market conditions. For standard products, 30 days is common. For custom manufacturing or volatile markets, shorter periods (15-30 days) are appropriate. Always clearly state the validity period on the document.

Do I need special software to create proforma invoices?

No, you don't need special software. You can use word processors, spreadsheets, or online invoice generators. The key is ensuring your proforma invoice looks professional and includes all necessary information. Many businesses find online tools more efficient for creating consistent, professional-looking documents.

Can proforma invoices be used for tax purposes?

Generally, proforma invoices cannot be used for tax deductions or VAT claims since they don't represent completed transactions. They're preliminary documents that don't create legal payment obligations. Only actual invoices for delivered goods or services can typically be used for tax purposes, but consult your accountant for specific guidance.

Proforma invoices are powerful tools for businesses engaged in complex sales processes, international trade, or custom manufacturing. When created professionally and used appropriately, they streamline transactions, reduce misunderstandings, and help both buyers and sellers manage expectations effectively. Remember to keep them detailed, professional, and clearly marked as preliminary documents to avoid confusion and maintain good customer relationships.
